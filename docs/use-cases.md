# Use Cases

## Remote Development

Access your development server from anywhere. Full terminal on your phone when you're away from your desk.

## Quick Commands

Run deployment scripts, check server status, or restart services from your mobile device.

## Learning & Teaching

Share terminal access with students or teammates. Multiple sessions, multiple devices.

## Home Lab Management

Manage your home server, Raspberry Pi, or media center from any device on your network.

---

## Why a Web Terminal?

A browser is the most universal client there is. A web terminal removes friction that traditional tools add:

- **No client needed.** Your phone, your partner's laptop, a Chromebook at a coffee shop — they all have a browser. No app to install, no keys to configure.
- **Passkey authentication.** No SSH keys to generate, copy, rotate, or debug. Katulong uses WebAuthn passkeys. Register once with your fingerprint, done.
- **Works over HTTPS.** No firewall rules, port forwarding, dynamic DNS, or VPN. Katulong works through any tunnel — same port, same protocol as every other website.
- **Persistent sessions.** Sessions persist by default. Close your browser, open it tomorrow, your session is there. No tmux keybindings to learn or `.tmux.conf` to configure.
- **Mobile-friendly.** Katulong has swipe navigation, a shortcut toolbar, and a full-screen text area that works with speech-to-text. No struggling with missing Ctrl, Tab, or arrow keys.
- **Drag-and-drop support.** A browser terminal handles drag-and-drop, clipboard, and file uploads natively because it's a browser.
- **Easy sharing.** Share a URL. Multiple people can join the same session instantly — open the same link and you're pair programming in real time. No credentials to distribute.
